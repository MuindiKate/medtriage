WORLD HEALTH ORGANIZATION - IMCI GUIDELINES
Integrated Management of Childhood Illness

DANGER SIGNS IN CHILDREN
General danger signs require immediate referral:
- Child cannot drink or breastfeed
- Child vomits everything
- Child has had convulsions
- Child is lethargic or unconscious

PNEUMONIA CLASSIFICATION
Severe Pneumonia (Emergency - Refer immediately):
- Chest indrawing present
- Oxygen saturation below 95%
- Respiratory rate above 50 breaths/min in children 2-12 months
- Respiratory rate above 40 breaths/min in children 1-5 years
- Nasal flaring, grunting
- Cyanosis

Non-Severe Pneumonia (Urgent - Treat with antibiotics):
- Fast breathing without chest indrawing
- Respiratory rate 50+ in infants, 40+ in older children
- No general danger signs

No Pneumonia (Routine):
- Cough or cold symptoms
- No fast breathing
- No chest indrawing

MALARIA TRIAGE
Severe Malaria (Emergency - Refer immediately):
- Prostration or inability to sit/stand
- Impaired consciousness
- Respiratory distress
- Multiple convulsions
- Abnormal bleeding
- Severe anaemia (pallor)
- Haemoglobinuria (black water)
- Jaundice

Uncomplicated Malaria (Urgent):
- Fever with positive RDT
- No severity signs
- Able to take oral medication

DEHYDRATION ASSESSMENT
Severe Dehydration (Emergency):
- Sunken eyes
- Skin pinch goes back very slowly (>2 seconds)
- Not able to drink
- Lethargic or unconscious

Some Dehydration (Urgent):
- Sunken eyes
- Skin pinch goes back slowly
- Drinks eagerly, thirsty

No Dehydration (Routine):
- No signs above
- Drinking normally

FEVER ASSESSMENT
High Risk Fever (Emergency):
- Temperature above 39.5°C with danger signs
- Stiff neck (meningitis risk)
- Bulging fontanelle in infants
- Petechial or purpuric rash
- Child under 3 months with any fever

Moderate Risk Fever (Urgent):
- Temperature 38.5-39.5°C
- No danger signs
- Source of infection identified

MALNUTRITION
Severe Acute Malnutrition (Emergency):
- MUAC below 115mm
- Bilateral pitting oedema
- Weight for height below -3 Z-score
- Medical complications present

Moderate Acute Malnutrition (Urgent):
- MUAC 115-125mm
- No oedema
- No medical complications

NEONATAL DANGER SIGNS (Emergency - refer all immediately):
- Stopped feeding well
- Convulsions
- Fast breathing (60+ breaths/min)
- Severe chest indrawing
- No spontaneous movement
- Fever or low temperature
- Jaundice in first 24 hours
