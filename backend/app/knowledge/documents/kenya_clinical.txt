KENYA CLINICAL GUIDELINES - EMERGENCY TRIAGE
Ministry of Health Kenya, 2016

TRIAGE LEVELS
RED (Emergency - Immediate):
Life-threatening conditions requiring immediate intervention.
Patient must be seen within minutes.

YELLOW (Urgent - Priority):
Serious conditions that are not immediately life-threatening.
Patient must be seen within 30 minutes.

GREEN (Routine - Non-urgent):
Minor conditions. Patient can wait.

BLACK (Expectant):
Injuries incompatible with survival given available resources.

CHEST PAIN ASSESSMENT
Emergency chest pain indicators:
- Crushing or pressure-like chest pain
- Pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or back
- Associated sweating, nausea, shortness of breath
- Signs of shock: low BP, rapid weak pulse
- Oxygen saturation below 90%

STROKE RECOGNITION (FAST):
Face drooping asymmetrically
Arm weakness - one arm drifts down
Speech difficulty - slurred or strange
Time to call for emergency referral immediately

SHOCK RECOGNITION
Signs of shock (Emergency):
- Systolic BP below 90mmHg
- Heart rate above 100 beats per minute
- Capillary refill time above 2 seconds
- Cold, clammy skin
- Altered mental status
- Urine output below 0.5ml/kg/hour

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES
Immediate referral required for:
- Heavy vaginal bleeding in pregnancy
- Severe headache with visual disturbance in pregnancy (eclampsia risk)
- Absent fetal movements
- Cord prolapse
- Shoulder dystocia
- Postpartum haemorrhage (blood loss >500ml)

VITAL SIGNS REFERENCE
Normal ranges by age:
Neonate (0-28 days): HR 100-160, RR 40-60, Temp 36.5-37.5
Infant (1-12 months): HR 100-160, RR 30-60, Temp 36.5-37.5
Toddler (1-3 years): HR 90-150, RR 24-40, Temp 36.5-37.5
Preschool (3-5 years): HR 80-140, RR 22-34, Temp 36.5-37.5
School age (6-12 years): HR 70-120, RR 18-30, Temp 36.5-37.5
Adult: HR 60-100, RR 12-20, Temp 36.5-37.5

SEPSIS RECOGNITION
SIRS Criteria (2 or more = sepsis risk):
- Temperature >38°C or <36°C
- Heart rate >90 beats per minute
- Respiratory rate >20 breaths per minute
- White blood cell count >12,000 or <4,000

Septic Shock (Emergency):
- Sepsis plus hypotension not responding to fluids
- Immediate IV access and fluid resuscitation
- Blood cultures before antibiotics
- Broad spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
